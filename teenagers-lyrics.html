About

The rock band My Chemical Romance, they were a popular American rock band that formed in New Jersey in 2001. The band consisted of lead vocalist Gerard Way, guitarists Ray Toro and Frank Iero, bassist Mikey Way, and drummer Bob Bryar (who was later replaced by Matt Pelissier).

My Chemical Romance’s music can be classified as alternative rock, punk rock, emo, and post-hardcore. They gained a large following with their second studio album, “Three Cheers for Sweet Revenge,” which was released in 2004. The album spawned hit singles such as “Helena” and “I’m Not Okay (I Promise),” and helped establish the band as an important figure in the emo/pop-punk scene of the mid-2000s.

After releasing several more successful albums, My Chemical Romance disbanded in 2013. However, in 2019, the band announced that they would be reuniting for a series of concerts.
